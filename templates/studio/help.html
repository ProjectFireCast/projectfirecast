FireCast User Guide
Introduction

FireCast is an online web application that allows users to Record, Edit Upload and Listen to podcasts.
This application was developed by a group of university students during the Project course offered in the Bachelor of Information technology. As a team, the students worked together in order to fulfil the clients’ requirements and needs for his overall vision for this project.
The client felt that there is an opportunity in the market when it comes to a good podcast website/community. FireCast aims to provide users with a platform where users can share their podcasts and really build a great community.
Users have a range of flexible options to log into the website such as using their Google Account, or simply just using an email and password. If users do not wish to sign up or login, the website is still accessible and users can record and edit their podcasts. Although, if users want to upload a podcast, an account must be created.

Installation instructions

This product is an online application meaning that you will not need to install any software. In order to use the website, ensure you have the following:

  • a computer or mobile device
  • a microphone
  • a compatible web browser (Google Chrome is recommended)
  • internet connection
Site Navigation

When you first enter the website, you are redirected onto the homepage which presents you the FireCast logo on a mixer background. You can use the buttons on the navigation bar to either log in or sign up, or you can scroll down for more options. If users decide to scroll down, you are presented with the option to create an account.

Registration

There are two options to register a FireCast account:

  • Email & Password
  • Google account

If you choose to create an account using your email and password it is advisable to first read our privacy policy. Once read, you will need to fill out the form below supplying your email and password, which must meet the following requirements:

  • Password must contain at least 8 characters
  • Password must not be too common
  • Password must be alphanumeric

If you decide to use your Google account, by logging into one of those accounts, your FireCast account will automatically be created.
You do not need an account to use the website, however: if you wish to upload and share your own podcasts an account is necessary.

Main Menu

Once your account has been created and/or you are logged in, the website will bring you to the menu screen in which users can select one of the following options:

  • Record – Selecting this option redirects them to the recording page, where users can create their own podcasts.
  • Listen – Selecting this option brings the user to a list of Podcasts that can be listened to and/or downloaded.
  • Upload – Selecting this option allows users to upload and share a podcast that they have created.

Note: Non-registered users can still record and listen to podcasts, but will need to register a new account or login in order to upload a new podcast.

Fade-in/fade-out effects

To add a fade effect to your audio, use the left arrow button (fade in) or the right arrow button (fade out). On the waveform/track, click where you want the fade to end/start. In the screen shots below, I have set the fade to end at approximately 10 seconds.

There are 4 different types of fade in and out to use. These include:

Logarithmic

The logarithmic fade sounds consistent and smooth since the perceived volume is increased over the whole duration of the fade.

Linear

A linear fade-in curve makes it sound as though the volume increases sharply at the beginning, and more gradually towards the end. The same principle applies on a fade-out where a gradual drop in volume can be perceived in the beginning, and the fade gets more abrupt towards the end.

Exponential

The exponential curve shape is in many ways the precise opposite of the logarithmic curves. The fade-in works as follows: it increases in volume slowly and then it shoots up very quickly at the end of the fade. The fade-out drops very quickly (from the maximum volume) and then declines slowly again over the duration of the fade.

S-Curve

The level of the sound is 50% at the midpoint, but before and after the midpoint the shape is not linear.

Listen to a podcast

This menu option allows you to view and play a list of podcasts that you have uploaded. You also have the option to download the podcast for offline listening. The information shown on this page includes, the podcast title, an image, the time and date the podcast was uploaded, and a description. By clicking on the title of the podcast, it redirects you to a page that provides you with more detail about that particular podcast if additional information is supplied.

Upload a Podcast

This option provides you with an upload form where you can upload a podcast. Information to be entered includes the title of the podcast, a description, an optional image and of course, your podcast audio file.
